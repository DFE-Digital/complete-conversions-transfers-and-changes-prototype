St. Wilfred's Primary School

External stakeholder kick-off

Have you sent the introduction emails?

Kick-off and introduction emails should be sent to the trust, school and their solicitors. Contact the local authority separately.

Introduction emails should include documents that schools and trusts will need to complete, as well as any important dates.
Recommended content for kick-off emails

Emails should include:

  • attached documents that school, trusts or their solicitors will need to complete and return
  • a list of who should complete each document, with an appropriate due date
  • a project end date and a list of key dates or milestones
  • suggested ways of working, such as monthly progress calls
  • link to useful websites and forms that need completing, such as the vendor account form
  • an explanation that the bank details they enter in the vendor account form are only valid for 4 months and we may need to ask them for bank details again
  • some suggested dates for the kick-off meeting

Have you agreed a date for the kick-off?
For example, 27 3 2007

Have you had the school and trust kick-off meeting?

Check that the school, trust and their solicitors have received all the documents they need.

Make sure they know who needs to complete the documents and when they need to return them.

Documents to check and questions to ask at the meeting

You should check that the:

  • school and trust have all the necessary documents
  • right people know they're responsible for completing certain documents and due dates
  • school has decided whether to have Risk Protection Agreement insurance or not
  • ways of working have been agreed
  • number of high needs places are confirmed
  • bank details have been provided for the conversion grant, if needed
  • grant payment amount is confirmed. Some schools may have tried to convert before so they may not have the full Â£25,000 available

Make sure schools or trusts are aware they will need to submit bank details separately for the General annual grant payment.

Have you contacted the local authority?
You should check that the local authority have received the land questionnaire and agree to the conversion date.
